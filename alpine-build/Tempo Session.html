Tempo Session

Workout Details

These should be done at maximal steady pace for the duration.

Thus a 5 minute tempo session might be slightly faster/harder than a 15 minute one.

In the program, these are sometimes noted as “Tempo 10”, meaning ten minutes at tempo pace, after warming up. They also get noted as something like “Tempo 10:5:10”, meaning 10 minutes at Tempo (Hr Zone 3) pace, then 5 minutes easy active recovery, then 10 minutes at Tempo.

These can be done by doing hard and fast uphill bits, faster running, hard cycling, ski sprints, and more. I expect that these will be placed within a CO workout. For example, you might go out for a hike on a local mountain trail. 20 minutes of CO finds you at the base of some steep switchbacks, so you pick up the pace to one that is sustainable but near your “red line.” You hold this pace for 5 minutes (your Tempo assignment for the day), then go back to easy conversational pace for the remainder of the workout.

Over time, your tempo pace will improve. You’ll be able to go faster/longer with heavier loads. For many athletes, this is the most profound feeling of better fitness with an alpine prep program.
